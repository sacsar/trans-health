How does healthcare work in the US?

In the US, most people get insurance through three main forms: an employer-provided plan, Medicare or Medicaid subsidized by the government, or an individual plan purchased through state insurance exchanges. What is an exclusion or excluded services?

Excluded services are health care services that your health insurance doesn't pay for or cover. Whether or not an insurance company will cover transition-related surgery differs largely by insurance, by plan, and by state. Some states have bans on insurance exclusions for transgender healthcare and some have inclusive health benefits for state employees. What is a formulary?

In the context of insurance, a formulary is the set of prescription drugs available to plan enrollees. Specific prescription drugs can be covered at different rates, where lower cost drugs may have lower cost sharing, e.g. 10% co-insurance (the amount of covered health care costs that the patient is responsible for, see glossary).
